Phishing is a a type of cybercrime in which attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trustworthy entity. It usually happens through emails, messages, or fake websites that look genuine.

A typical phishing attack follows these steps. First, the attacker creates a fake message that appears to come from a trusted source, such as a bank or popular service. Next, the message includes a link directing the user to a fake website that closely resembles the original. Then, the victim is urged to act quickly, often with warnings like "account will be blocked" or "urgent verification required". When the victim enters their login details or OTPs, the attacker captures this information. Finally, the attacker uses this credentials to access the victim's account and perform unauthorized transactions and data theft.

Phishing attacks commonly target students, working professionals, and elderly users who may not verify links carefully. These attacks are especially effective on people who are less aware of online security practice.

The consequence of phishing can be severe. Victims may lose money, personal data, or access to important accounts. It can also lead to identity theft and long-term financial or emotional stress. Therefore, awareness and caution are essential to prevent such cybercrimes.

Additionally, phishing attacks continue to evolve with more advanced techniques such as personalized messages and realistic website designs, making them harder to detect. Users must stay alert, verify sources before sharing information, and use security measures like two-factor authentication to reduce the risk of falling victim to such scams.
